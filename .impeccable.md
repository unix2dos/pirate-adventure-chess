## Design Context

### Users
- Primary users are children and parents playing together on one screen.
- The game should also work for casual players who want a light, quick local multiplayer experience.
- The core job to be done is simple: start fast, understand the goal immediately, enjoy short bursts of surprise, and finish a round without needing to study rules.

### Brand Personality
- Playful, bright, adventurous.
- The emotional target is excitement without stress: a game that feels friendly, funny, and easy to read.
- The tone should feel like a pirate-themed party adventure, not a serious naval strategy game.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Use a cartoon amusement-park take on pirate adventure.
- Prefer bright tropical colors, playful shapes, and exaggerated feedback over dark ocean realism or parchment-map nostalgia.
- The board should feel like an adventure route through themed islands and sea zones rather than a plain numbered spreadsheet.
- Anti-references: dashboard-like sidebars, developer-tool visuals, dark blue demo styling, generic modal-heavy flows, and static configuration-form start screens.

### Design Principles
1. Make the next action obvious in under two seconds.
2. Sell adventure through places and encounters, not through text logs.
3. Keep rules shallow, but make feedback rich and celebratory.
4. Optimize for local pass-and-play first; keep code boundaries ready for future online multiplayer.
5. Every major screen should look like a game scene, not an admin panel.
